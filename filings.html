Filing types, in plain terms

The forms this digest watches, and what each one means. Definitions follow SEC usage; read the SEC forms index for the full detail.

10-K

Annual report

The annual report for U.S. filers. Audited financial statements, a description of the business, and risk factors.

10-Q

Quarterly report

The quarterly report for U.S. filers. Unaudited financial statements for the quarter.

8-K

Current report

covered

Discloses material events between periodic reports. Companies must file within four business days of the event.

20-F

Annual report, foreign issuer

The annual report for foreign private issuers listed on U.S. exchanges. Many shipping companies file this instead of a 10-K.

40-F

Annual report, Canadian issuer

The annual report for certain Canadian issuers under the U.S.–Canada multijurisdictional system.

6-K

Interim report, foreign issuer

An interim report from a foreign private issuer. Press releases and shareholder communications often arrive as 6-Ks.

4

Insider transaction report

covered

A statement of changes in beneficial ownership. Directors, officers and 10% owners must report their trades within two business days.

What this is

An automated research digest. An AI model drafts each note from the text of a public SEC filing. A separate program then checks every quote and figure against that filing and withholds anything that fails the check.
