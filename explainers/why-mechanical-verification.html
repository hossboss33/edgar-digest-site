Why this site verifies quotes mechanically

AI drafts every note here. A dumb program decides what publishes. That order is deliberate.

By the Filings Desk · evergreen

Language models are good at reading long documents and bad at admitting what they did not read. The predictable failure is a summary that sounds right, cites a plausible number, and is wrong. Any publication that automates drafting has to answer for that failure mode before it publishes a word.

This site answers it with the least clever tool available: string comparison. The model must attach an exact quote to every factual bullet. A separate program then checks that the quote appears in the filing, character for character, and that every number in the bullet appears inside its own quote. No judgment, no thresholds, no model checking a model.

What fails the check is quarantined, and the quarantine is not small. On a typical heavy day, a meaningful share of drafted bullets fail: a paraphrased quote, a date restated in a different format, a number the filing implies but never states. None of it publishes.

The check has honest limits, and the methodology page lists them. A verbatim quote can still mislead out of context. A note can pass every check and omit the most important fact in the filing. Mechanical verification sets a floor, not a ceiling, which is why every note links to the primary source.

The order of operations is the whole design: the creative system proposes, the dumb system disposes.

What this is

An automated research digest. An AI model drafts each note from the text of a public SEC filing. A separate program then checks every quote and figure against that filing and withholds anything that fails the check.

This is general information and commentary, not investment advice, and not a recommendation to buy or sell any security. The publisher is not a registered investment adviser or broker-dealer. A verbatim quote can still mislead out of context, so read the primary source: every note links to the official filing on sec.gov. No warranty of accuracy or completeness.
