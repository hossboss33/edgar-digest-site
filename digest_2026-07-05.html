The BriefWritten by the machine, only from the verified notes below

Boardrooms did most of the filing this week. Consolidated Edison elected Tali Farhadian Weinstein as a director effective July 1 and seated her on two committees, including safety and operations oversight. Prologis determined that its new director Kelly meets NYSE and SEC independence standards and placed him on its governance committee. Williams assigned new directors Robb Turner and Lloyd Helms to audit, compensation, and environmental committees, with a $130,000 annual cash retainer and equity awards that carry a mandatory one-year deferral.

Executive transitions ran alongside the board moves. Ajay Puri, a 21-year NVIDIA executive, notified the company on June 28 that he intends to retire as Executive Vice President of Worldwide Field Operations; he stays on in a senior advisory role during the transition. At Enterprise Products, the filing traces Fowler's path to the top job: Co-Chief Executive alongside Teague since January 2020, CFO before that.

Credit desks were busy into the quarter turn. Cheniere Energy raised commitments under its revolving credit facility by $500 million to $1.75 billion and pushed the maturity out a year to August 2031. Nasdaq signed a new revolver it can expand by up to $1.0 billion, with a leverage covenant of 3.75 to 1.00 and no loans outstanding on day one. Analog Devices disclosed terms that let it convert outstanding loans to a one-year term loan for a 0.50% fee, alongside an EBITDA-to-interest covenant of 3.00 to 1.00.

Capital structures moved too. Digital Realty completed its acquisition of Blackstone's interests in two joint ventures, and Blackstone sold 12,310,249 Digital Realty shares at $185.00 in an underwritten offering; Digital Realty received none of the proceeds. Dell Technologies converted from a Delaware corporation to a Texas corporation, one share for one share across all three stock classes, and stockholders re-elected Michael Dell with 3,404,074,505 votes in favor.

One vote went the other way. Aon shareholders rejected the advisory executive compensation resolution, 110,798,636 votes against to 69,888,299 in favor, and the company extended CEO Gregory Case's international assignment letter through June 2027.
